Frequently Asked Questions

What Is Fantasy Run For A Million?

What is Fantasy Run For A Million?

Fantasy Run For A Million is a free-to-play fan engagement game built around western performance horse competition. Participants build a fantasy team by selecting riders from reining, cow horse, and cutting disciplines, then earn fantasy points based on how their selected riders finish in The Run For A Million event. It follows the same format fans of fantasy sports in other professional sports already know — applied to western horsemanship.

Who can play?

Any fan of western performance horse sports can participate. The game is free to enter and requires no prior fantasy sports experience — only an interest in reining, cow horse, and cutting competition.

Is this affiliated with The Run For A Million?

No. Fantasy Run For A Million is an independent fan engagement platform and is not affiliated with, sponsored by, or officially connected to The Run For A Million or its organizers. Competition result data is sourced from publicly available results.

How the Fantasy Format Works

How do I build a fantasy team?

Browse the eligible rider database across all three disciplines. Select riders for each discipline slot on your roster — 2 reining, 2 cow horse, 2 cutting, and 1 bonus rider. Your team's fantasy points are determined by how your selected riders finish in their classes at The Run For A Million. When contest entry opens, you'll submit your official team through the Pick Your Team page. If you're new to the game, the fantasy strategy guide walks through how to evaluate riders in each discipline before you build your roster.

When does team entry open?

Contest entry opens before The Run For A Million begins. The exact date will be announced here and on the Pick Your Team page. In the meantime, you can explore the rider database, try the demo team builder, and read the fantasy strategy guide to get familiar with how to evaluate riders in each discipline.

Can I change my team after submitting?

The contest entry rules — including whether teams can be edited after submission — will be published in full when entry opens. Check the How It Works page for the most current information.

Fantasy Scoring

How are fantasy points calculated?

Points are earned based on your riders' finishing positions in their discipline classes at The Run For A Million. 1st place earns 100 points, 2nd earns 80, 3rd earns 65, 4th earns 50, 5th earns 40, 6th–10th earn 25, and any qualified completed run earns 10 points. Bonus point events may award additional points for standout performances. See the full scoring rules for point values by position, or read the understanding scoring guide for how the point structure should inform your rider selection.

What are bonus point events?

Bonus points are additional scoring opportunities on top of base placement points. Proposed bonus categories include a discipline winner bonus (+25 pts), highest composite score bonus (+20 pts), comeback rider bonus (+15 pts), rookie/underdog bonus (+15 pts), and a fan favorite bonus (+10 pts). All bonus categories will be confirmed before contest entry opens.

What if two riders tie in the official results?

Tiebreaker rules will be published as part of the full contest terms before entry opens. In general, ties in official competition results are handled according to the governing body's rules for that discipline.

Riders and Disciplines

What disciplines are included?

Fantasy Run For A Million covers three western performance horse disciplines: reining, cow horse, and cutting. Each discipline has its own eligible rider roster and scoring structure.

How are riders selected for the eligible roster?

The rider roster reflects competitors participating in The Run For A Million event. As the event field is finalized, the eligible rider database will be updated accordingly. All 24 current roster members are listed in the rider database.

Can I pick riders from multiple disciplines?

Yes. Your roster includes slots for reining, cow horse, and cutting riders, plus a bonus pick from any discipline. You can concentrate on one discipline or spread across all three. The team building guide covers how to think about allocating picks across disciplines and using the bonus slot effectively.

Are there individual rider profile pages?

Yes, for the currently profiled riders. Andrea Fappani, Casey Deary, Cade McCutcheon, Corey Cushing, Boyd Rice, Chris Dawson, Adan Banuelos, Beau Galyean, and Austin Shepard all have profile pages. The remaining 15 riders will be profiled as the platform develops.

About the Platform

Is this free to play?

Yes. Fantasy Run For A Million is completely free to participate in. There is no entry fee, no paid subscription, and no money changes hands between players. It is a fan knowledge competition scored on real event results.

Is this a gambling or betting platform?

No. Fantasy Run For A Million does not involve gambling, wagering, sportsbook activity, or financial betting of any kind. Participants compete on knowledge of western performance horse sport — the leaderboard ranks fans by fantasy points, not money.

How do I contact the platform?

Visit the Contact page for ways to reach the Fantasy Run For A Million team with questions, feedback, or rider data inquiries.
