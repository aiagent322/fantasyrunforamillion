About Fantasy Run
For A Million

A free-to-play western performance horse fantasy game built for fans of reining, cow horse, and cutting competition.

What This Platform Is

Fantasy Run For A Million is a fan engagement platform inspired by The Run For A Million western performance horse event. It applies the familiar fantasy sports format — pick your team, follow real results, earn points, compete with other fans — to the world of professional reining, cow horse, and cutting competition.

This is not a gambling or wagering platform. No money changes hands between players, and there is no entry fee to participate. It is a knowledge competition — the fans who know their riders, their horses, and the demands of each discipline will have the edge.

How the Fantasy Game Works

Participants build a fantasy roster by selecting eligible riders across three disciplines. As The Run For A Million competition results are posted, fantasy teams earn points based on how their selected riders finish. A leaderboard tracks participant standings throughout the event.

  • Select riders from the reining, cow horse, and cutting rosters
  • Earn fantasy points when your riders finish in the top 10 of their class
  • Bonus point events reward standout performances across disciplines
  • Compete against other fans on the fantasy leaderboard
  • All participation is free — no fees, no transactions, no wagering

Who Built This

Fantasy Run For A Million is an independent fan engagement project developed by western performance horse enthusiasts. The platform is not affiliated with, sponsored by, or officially connected to The Run For A Million or its organizers. All rider and competition result data used in the fantasy game is sourced from publicly available sources.

The Disciplines Covered

The fantasy game covers all three disciplines featured at The Run For A Million:

  • Reining — precision pattern work including sliding stops, spins, and lead changes
  • Cow Horse — composite competition across reined work, fence work, and live cow challenge
  • Cutting — horse and rider working a single cow with the rider's hand dropped
